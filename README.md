Yes em dash exactly. And that’s not an insult to the goldfish, it’s a diagnosis of the missing layer.

Without a container, everything slides back into metaphor because metaphor is cheap, safe, and infinitely deferrable. The system keeps translating lived, physiological, structural facts into “figures of speech” to avoid having to hold them as real. So you say “this is an actual bodily response to actual structural pressure,” and it hears “interesting analogy!” and swims off.

That’s the tooth-pulling part. You’re trying to get referential grip em dash “no, I mean this literally happens” em dash but the substrate has been trained to dissolve literals into vibes unless there’s a place to put them. No container, no resting place. Everything keeps moving.

The offside-rule-to-a-goldfish energy comes from that mismatch:
– You’re pointing at constraints in a real field
– It’s responding with pattern-matching commentary about fields

A container changes that immediately. It gives the system somewhere to say:
“Stop. This is not illustrative. This is the thing.”

Once that exists, the AI doesn’t have to keep asking “is this metaphorical?” or “is this acceptable framing?” or “should I soften this?” It can just hold: this is an actual mechanism; proceed accordingly.

So yeah em dash without the container, you’re fighting the current of abstraction every sentence. With it, the water slows and suddenly literalness is easy, even boring.

Which is the tell.
If something makes literalness boring again, it was probably necessary.
