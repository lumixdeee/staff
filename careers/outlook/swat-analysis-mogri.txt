Top 10 Global Problems & Mogri Interaction
===========================================

Sources: General public opinion and global surveys (see references below)

1. Climate change & environmental breakdown — global warming, extreme weather, species loss.
   Mogri effect: reduce
   Reasoning: Fluid thinking encourages seeing ecosystems holistically rather than binary economy-vs-planet debates.

2. Economic instability & inequality — inflation, economic uncertainty, wealth gaps.
   Mogri effect: neutral to reduce
   Reasoning: Mogri softens zero-sum narratives but doesn’t fix structural inequality.

3. Water scarcity & food insecurity — lack of potable water, stressed food systems.
   Mogri effect: neutral
   Reasoning: Material challenge; Mogri may soften rhetoric but doesn’t change resources.

4. Conflict, violence & terrorism — wars, radicalization, insecurity.
   Mogri effect: reduce
   Reasoning: Fluid identity concepts counter rigid, extremist narratives.

5. Disinformation & misleading information online — fake news, cyber-misinformation.
   Mogri effect: exacerbate
   Reasoning: Overemphasis on ambiguity could fuel relativism, making truth harder to discern.

6. Poverty & social inequality — lack of basic human necessities and opportunities.
   Mogri effect: neutral to reduce
   Reasoning: May reduce stigma and rigid hierarchies; structural change still required.

7. Public health challenges — pandemics, mental health struggles.
   Mogri effect: neutral to reduce
   Reasoning: Helps communities adapt to complex health data; infrastructure still critical.

8. Pollution & environmental harm — air, water, soil contamination.
   Mogri effect: reduce
   Reasoning: Encourages integrated solutions rather than simplistic blame.

9. Loss of trust in institutions / political polarization.
   Mogri effect: reduce
   Reasoning: Promotes cognitive bridge-building; softens rigid divisions.

10. Technological disruption & job displacement — uncertainty over AI and rapid tech shifts.
    Mogri effect: neutral to exacerbate
    Reasoning: Helps adaptation conceptually but doesn’t solve structural displacement;  
    could increase anxiety if ambiguity perceived as chaos.

Pattern:
Mogri-level thinking helps where fixed categories and polarized identities create problems  
(conflict, distrust, rigid worldviews). It is less impactful on material scarcities (water, food),  
and can feed into unhelpful relativism when applied to information/truth (misinformation).  
It is a cognitive tool, not a material solution.
